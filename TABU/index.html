Employment rates of recent graduates over time

More precise data reveals that the employment rate for recent postsecondary graduates dropped to its lowest point in 2014 (79.5%). The percentage increased to 85.0% in 2019 before declining in 2020 when the COVID-19 pandemic started to spread. In 2021, the level increased once more, reaching an almost-previous level of 84.9% before the COVID-19 pandemic.
Regarding recent medium-level graduates, the EU employment rate was at its lowest in 2013 (68.4 %). By contrast, the highest share for this subpopulation was recorded at 76.0 % in 2018. In 2020 there was a rapid decrease due to the COVID-19 pandemic, but the share rose again in 2021 to a level of 73.1 %.

Professional burnouts

Most adults spend the majority of their time at work. Given that most of maturity is spent at work, workplace wellbeing is becoming more crucial to overall well-being.
Professional burnout is a syndrome of emotional tiredness, depersonalization or cynicism, and a poor sense of personal success as a reaction to ongoing work stress. This stands in contrast to well-being. Professional burnout has an impact on a person's capacity to arrange the particular difficulties occurring at different stages of professional growth, particularly those that arise when a human transitions from the role of learner to that of apprentice to early-career professional. Depression, coping with alcohol or drug use, broken relationships, professional mistakes, decreasing job satisfaction, and intention to quit the job - are all effects of burnout.
